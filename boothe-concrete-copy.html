Positive Affirmation Page

A non-profit resource center needs an easy to use and easy to distribute tool to aid their admission process and empower clients to reflect upon desired areas of healing and growth.

Opportunities:

Research showed many opportunities for feature improvements on their current tool, many including accessibility and ease of use.

Live Site -->

Who's the User?

PAIN POINTS:

Client

Overwhelmed with content overload
Fear of answering question wrong
Exhausted with already lengthy admission process and forms

owner

Sad to be using client tool that does not match their curriculum
Rushed to get better intake process before curriculum expands
Fear of being design being misrepresented by poor build implementation

SERVICE 
PROVIDER

Concern of overwhelming clients with long forms
Annoyed at having to restart process with any error
Pressured to avoid mistake for fear of having to restart process

The Research

USER INTERVIEWS:

Template 1

  • Owners need a tool that reflects evidence based counseling methods with clinically backed verbiage.
  • Counselors need a tool that reflects evidence based practices and matches their current resource availability.
  • 'If I've got to fill out a form, I hope it's not long' - Users prioritize ease of use in form submission.

HYPOTHESIS:

Template 2

Users will feel more empowered through form submission if tools are added to allow for more error prevention and correction.

By prioritizing strategic chunking methods, easing transitions of animated/moving content, and adding clearer information hierarchy, users will feel less overwhelmed and a greater sense of engagement and ease through intake process.

The Design . . .

So, you will notice that the final product and initial design varried significantly. There was a change in project priorities, which I will explain below....

First Usability Study. . .

Template 2

Pain Points

  • Users were less likely to navigate to nav menu items to right of screen and often hesitated upon selecting nav dropdown
  • Initial design of buttons did not appear selectable (sorry, don't have a pic of the initial design - you are seeing the revised version), so users were selecting multiple times in order to open individual projects
  • Nav menu horizontal width was discouraging navigation to some right nav items.
  • Confusion when navigating between 'projects' and 'gallery'.
  • Hesitation upon home page an hesitation navigating between pages.
  • Perceived clashing of fonts on homepage, when nav menu is open.

Design Solutions

  • Switched to vertical navigation menu with better contrast. Editted user flow to reflect more intentional information architecture.
  • Added size and contrast to buttons and made surrounding section selectable on mobile versions.
  • Renamed nav menu items to reflection user priorities.
  • Changed nav button to add contrast and identified blue as 'call to action' color to clearly identify selectable items. This included changing button color, size, and weight.
  • Added buttons to navigate between most common pages (and pages to funnel clients to) towards bottom of section, so users don't have to reopen the nav menu to navigate from current page.
  • Changed weight of nav menu items and sub-items to better refine visual hierarchy and improve sense of consistency.

Second Usability Study. . .

Template 2

Pain Points

  • Users too many images/project page. Users were bored by the time they got to the end of each project gallery.
  • Abundance of negative space incited boredom and left users with a sense of uncertainty of page end point when scrolling.
  • Confusion regarding what this company does, who it serves, specificity of services. Users reported uncertainty of where to find this information from navigation menu.

Design Solutions

  • Varried image display by consilateing some image to a 3 column view. This decreased page length and added sense of variety to project gallery.
  • Decreased padding on section elements to consolidate information consistently throughout site.
  • Added content addressing these questions, editing chunking strategy on home page, and altered verbiage on nav menu to add 'about us' rather than 'services' page.

KPIs

Through the usability studies in this project, I measured SUS as my main KPI. While this indicated improvement on the tested paths, my sample size was not high enough for my later studies to reasonably indicate success.

I've just updated the privacy practices and installed Google Analytics for the site (1/20/23).

I will be assessing conversion rates, click paths, bounce and exit pages/rates, organic/inorganic searches, page times, and user demographics to measure whether the content on the site is attracting the target populations.

I'll also be looking at exit rate and click paths to the contact page, as well as getting feedback on referral rates from site in order to more accurately assess site performance.

I've got a few ideas in my mind for improvements, depending on what the analytics bring back.

I'll keep you posted on what I find!
