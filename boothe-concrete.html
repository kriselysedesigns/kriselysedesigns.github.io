Project Scope

Boothe Concrete is a best-in-its class architectural and board for concrete company in Austin TX who was in need of a website revamp! While the company has an excellent reputation in the area for providing the best quality concrete pours in the Austin area, they have traditionally acquired almost all of their business from strong relationships with local builders, architects, and project managers.

The concern is that their current web presence was not reflecting their quality of work, and that the impression from their current website was dissuading future customers from working with them.

While Boothe Concrete has become the expert in the area for one type of concrete, they have found themselves becoming so good that that type, that it is no longer challenging and interesting to Boothe's builders. Boothe Concrete wants a website that will attract more complex and interesting projects, as well as convey that they provide more services than the one they are feeling pigeon holed into.

Research showed many opportunities for feature improvements on the website. However, given the referral base nature of the industry, stakeholders agreed that the objective of this initial design was exclusively to more accurately represent the quality of work produced by the business (so that new clients would be more willing to give the project managers on the job the 'okay' to go with Boothe), and funned current and potential customers to contact Boothe Concrete.

Who's the User??

PAIN POINTS

Owner
  • Attracting out-of-scope business because of 'mom and pop shop' current web presence.
  • Bored with current projects.
  • Annoyed when people contact them about services they don't provide.
  • Fear of being sued if he charges for consultations, which he otherwise enjoys.
Architects
  • Annoyed with communication challenges throughout the build.
  • Stretched with needing to adjust with time/material limitations.
  • Fear of being design being misrepresented by poor implementation
Clients
  • Fear of being ripped off or over charged
  • Frustration with projects being slowed down and increasing cost
  • Confused: what are all these professionals that we need to pay for?
  • Overwhelmed with surprised timelines, barriers, and cost of build.
Project managers
  • Frustrated when clients don't go with their recommendation of industry professionals.
  • Fear of project going long/increasing cost.
  • Stuck when unable to find course requirements.
  • Overwhelmed when looking at new platform for the first time.
  • Inadequate for not being able to fix things for child.
  • Angry when help is not available immediately if course produces error.

USER GOALS

Owner

As an owner of concrete service trying to increase the quality of projects I accept, I need an elegant and professional looking website that displays my ability to succeed in complex projects because I need both industry partners and potential clients to think of me for more complex projects.

Architect

As an architect with a deep professional network, I need to know that the concrete service that I recommend for this complex project will accurately represent my design because I want to keep the respect and reputation of reliability within the construction and design industries, as well as avoid any lawsuits.

Client

As a successful business professional needing a home built, I need to completely trust the building team to provide quality work and a reliable build because I need to know that my family is falling asleep under reliable construction at night.

Project managers

As a project manager responsible for the outcome (timeline, cost, durability, legal qualifications) of this complex build, I need my team to be reliable, qualified, and communicative because I need to avoid pushed back deadlines and surprise costs to myself and the client.

First Usability Study. . .

Template 2

Pain Points

  • Users were less likely to navigate to nav menu items to right of screen and often hesitated upon selecting nav dropdown
  • Initial design of buttons did not appear selectable (sorry, don't have a pic of the initial design - you are seeing the revised version), so users were selecting multiple times in order to open individual projects
  • Nav menu horizontal width was discouraging navigation to some right nav items.
  • Confusion when navigating between 'projects' and 'gallery'.
  • Hesitation upon home page an hesitation navigating between pages.
  • Perceived clashing of fonts on homepage, when nav menu is open.

Design Solutions

  • Switched to vertical navigation menu with better contrast. Editted user flow to reflect more intentional information architecture.
  • Added size and contrast to buttons and made surrounding section selectable on mobile versions.
  • Renamed nav menu items to reflection user priorities.
  • Changed nav button to add contrast and identified blue as 'call to action' color to clearly identify selectable items. This included changing button color, size, and weight.
  • Added buttons to navigate between most common pages (and pages to funnel clients to) towards bottom of section, so users don't have to reopen the nav menu to navigate from current page.
  • Changed weight of nav menu items and sub-items to better refine visual hierarchy and improve sense of consistency.
