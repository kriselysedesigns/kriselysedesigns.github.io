Discovery

Project Goals:
Improve case management workflow, allowing clinicians to spend more time with clients and less time struggling in front of the computer screen. Solution must be heavily secure, as sensitive and protected client data is passed through.

Since clinicians must split their attention between clients and form submission tool at time of use, the intake assistance tools must be easy to use and require minimal thought.
‍
Target Audience:
Clients, case managers, nurses, and supervisors of crisis department.

Competitor Analysis:
While there are many specific online tools available to bridge many gaps in workflow, they require a high degree of 'tech saviness' and would require users to pass client into through 3rd party systems (so absolutely not usable).

Initial UX Research:

User Interviews:
Conducted usability study of initial form and user interviews with trauma informed clinicians prior to design process.

FEATURE PRIORITIZATION
Mapped interview findings, sorted by prevalence and priority of issue, and pitched changes to immediate supervisor and select clinicians.
‍
INSIGHTS:
Some experienced clinicians are unable to complete parts of the intake process due to small font sizes and finicky editing tools. Users want more error prevention and less room to 'mess up' form submissions.

Design

INPUT OPTIONS
Assuming users are already experiencing decision fatigue from an extensive intake process leading up to this form, we decreased input options from 10 to 4. This eliminates the tendency of disinterested users selecting only the middle option (ie, selecting 3 on 1-5 inputs) to avoid decision making.
‍
Color palette
‍I chose cool and calming colors as the prominent colors for the intake process, as seeking help is already a vulnerable and potentially emotional decision. Out of respect for client's need for ease of use, orange was chosen as accent for button submission and minimum necessary information needed by user to complete task.

SKIP SECTION
‍Clinician feedback informed need for customizable form structure, in order to honor unique needs and priorities of users.

BACK BUTTN
‍As survivors of trauma seek help, many users experience a sense of disempowerment over what happened to them. Increase sense of autonomy through form submission was an essential part of this design.‍
