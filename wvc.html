Positive Affirmation Page

I am creating tie project as part of the Google UX certification course through Coursera. The scope of this project is to research, design, and test an application to help families coordinate to-do-list items with a local wedding venue.

TOOLS:

Thought this project, I am using
-Userbit for user interviews and documentation,
-Maze for usability testing (both moderated and unmoderated),
-Adobe Illustrator and Google's Material.io for graphics, Miro for site maps, user flows, and affinity maps,
- Figma for the design and prototypes.
I keep track of progress in Asana along the way!

Positive Affirmation Page

Who's the User??

PAIN POINTS

Venue
  • Late payments from clients
  • Difficulty coordinating with vendors due to lack of info from clients
  • Unhappy clients and poor online ratings due to disorganization.
Hosts
  • Overwhelmed with understanding deadlines and prioritizing tasks.
  • Ambiguity from support system regarding level of commitment to tasks.
  • Tension within couple while juggling due dates an budgets.
  • Fear of missing a deadline.
  • Feel alone when unable to get in touch with anyone for help.
Family
  • Hurt feelings from lack of communication about due-dates.
  • Feeling stuck because not sure of how to be helpful.
  • Discomfort with frustrations of engaged couple.
Friends
  • Spending more time trying to understand what needs to be done than helping.
  • Unappreciated when engaged couple declines help.
  • Stretched when unable to contact engage couple to update on task completion.

USER GOALS:

Venue

As venue manager trying to make everyone happy, I need an oragnized and easy-to-use system, in which my staff, clients, and client support systems can stay on the same page with to-do-items because I want my clients to be happy and my business to thrive.

HOSTS

As an engaged individual, I need a clear and concise system that will let me coordinate to-do-items with my friends and family because I don't have the attention to be on top of everything due .

Family

As family member wanting to help, I need a way to know where to pick up with half completed tasks because I want to be helpful but don't want to take time from my engaged family member in order to understand where to start.

Friends

As a friend with exceptional design skills, I need a system where I can be delegated specific tasks without needing to understand the big picture because I want to be helpful with the bouquet arrangement, but don't have the time to commit to larger tasks.

BUT, in testing . . .

I performed moderated usabiliy studies on the prototype.

Some curious patterns arose:

  • Test participants were not able to identify use of the grid column layout of the to-do-list.
  • Participants went backwards on the itemized view of a test, despite being on the 'happy path' to complete their task.
  • Participants hesitated and reported confusion on the pages after closing the 'delegate' option.
